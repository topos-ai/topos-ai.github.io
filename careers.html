About Topos

Topos revolutionizes the ability to target and make decisions based on location—creating a holistic understanding of neighborhoods and cities.

Combining heterogeneous data from hundreds of data sources with advanced AI techniques, our products enable users to understand hard to quantify cultural aspects like how vibrant nightlife is, how urban or suburban an area feels, or how ‘foodie’ a neighborhood is. It also answers cross-geographic questions such as “What is the Williamsburg of Boston?”

Senior Researcher

For our research team we are hiring a senior researcher to focus on understanding location and cities in new ways using techniques from urban studies, design and architecture. In this role you will help us build out the research capability of Topos.

Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

  • Study built environment, streets, buildings and other elements in the city to come up with new ways of understanding the flow in the city
  • Proactively identifying new metrics to understand and analyze location
  • Building logical and practical solutions to construct and deploy metrics which might be useful for real estate, retail, travel, hospitality, urban planning and other location related fields.

Experience required:

  • 3+ years industry experience and a Master's degree in architecture or related fields
  • Proven ability to succeed in both collaborative and independent work environments
  • Strong critical thinker who enjoys and is able to work with both technical and non-technical colleagues
  • Strong research and analytical skills including the ability to gather, interpret and effectively communicate macro and micro urban data, trends and insights, with a keen interest in architecture and urbanism.
  • Ability to clearly present complex findings verbally, visually and in writing
  • Ability to construct functional reports to communicate findings and research
  • Manage, process and analyse large datasets with Python, R and other programming languages
  • Knowledge of D3, Matplotlib, Tableau or similar data visualization tools
  • Develop forecasting models to predict future global trends in cities, real estate, and urban planning
  • Publication of prior research in academic, peer-reviewed conferences and journals
  • Discern appropriate research methods, apply them effectively, through well-designed studies, and derive “actionable” knowledge
  • Research design, with ability to translate a minimally defined stakeholder interests into robust studies that will yield high quality, unbiased data
